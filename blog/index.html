    On average reward criterion

    At first view, the average-reward Markov Decision Process (MDP) feels like the natural choice, especially next to discounted formulations that often need extra real-world justification. In practice, though, the average-criterion approach hasn’t become as popular: subtle technical assumptions, multichain behavior, and delicate limit arguments make it less straightforward than it appears.
